9th Grade
Foundations of the Building Trades
Every student takes this core year — no track selection required. Covers safety, tools, math, blueprint reading, and hands-on exposure to all four trades. Students identify their primary interest by year-end.
Trades Safety & OSHA-10
Semester 1 · Required
▾

The foundational safety course every student completes before touching any tool or entering any lab. Covers OSHA regulations, hazard recognition, and PPE — culminating in the nationally recognized OSHA-10 Construction card.

  • Recognizing and reporting workplace hazards
  • PPE selection, fit, and maintenance
  • Electrical, fall, struck-by, and caught-in hazards
  • OSHA rights, responsibilities, and inspection process
  • Job site safety culture and reporting procedures
🏅 Earns: OSHA-10 Construction Certification
Construction Math & Measurement
Semester 1–2 · Required
▾

Applied math taught entirely in the context of construction — not abstract algebra. Students learn to measure, calculate, and problem-solve the way real tradespeople do on job sites.

  • Fractions, decimals, and unit conversion
  • Area, volume, and perimeter calculations
  • Reading a tape measure to 1/16 inch
  • Estimating material quantities
  • Applied geometry: angles, slopes, and the Pythagorean theorem
Core · All Tracks
Blueprint Reading & Technical Drawing
Semester 1–2 · Required
▾

Students learn to read and interpret construction documents — the universal language of every building trade. Covers residential and light commercial drawings with hands-on sketch exercises.

  • Floor plans, elevations, sections, and details
  • Reading scales and dimensions
  • Architectural, structural, electrical, and plumbing symbols
  • Spec sheets and material schedules
  • Introduction to digital plan viewing tools
Core · All Tracks
Intro to All Four Trades (Rotating)
Semester 2 · 4-week rotations
▾

A structured 16-week rotation giving every student hands-on exposure to all four trades before committing to a track. Taught in the school's trade lab with real tools and materials.

  • Electrical: wire a basic circuit, identify components
  • Plumbing: cut and join pipe, assemble a simple fixture
  • HVAC: identify system components, basic airflow concepts
  • Carpentry: measure, cut, and assemble a framed panel
  • Daily reflection journal — students track interest and aptitude
  • End-of-rotation: student selects preferred trade for 10th grade
Core · Leads to Track Selection
Hand & Power Tools Lab
Semester 1–2 · Required
▾

Hands-on competency with the most common hand and power tools used across all building trades. Students complete safety checkouts before using each tool class independently.

  • Hammers, screwdrivers, pliers, wrenches, and levels
  • Circular saw, jigsaw, and reciprocating saw
  • Power drill and impact driver
  • Proper storage, maintenance, and inspection of tools
  • Tool safety checkout system — sign-off per tool class
Core · All Tracks
OSHA-10 Certification
End of 9th grade · Milestone
▾

Upon completing the Trades Safety course, students sit for and earn the OSHA-10 Construction certification — recognized by virtually every employer and union in the trades.

  • Required for 10th grade lab access
  • Recognized by IBEW, UA, UBC, and most open-shop contractors
  • Card is valid indefinitely — students keep it for life
🏅 Credential Milestone
10th Grade
Trade Selection + Core Skills
Students choose their primary trade and begin formal technical training. Shared professional skills coursework runs alongside all tracks.
Electrical I
Electrical track · Year 1
▾

Introduction to electrical theory and residential wiring. Students begin working with real wire, boxes, and devices in a controlled lab environment aligned with NCCER Electrical Level 1.

  • Ohm's Law, voltage, current, and resistance
  • Series and parallel circuits
  • NEC codebook orientation and table navigation
  • Residential wiring: outlets, switches, and lighting circuits
  • Box fill calculations and conduit type introduction
⚡ Electrical Track
Plumbing I
Plumbing track · Year 1
▾

Foundational plumbing skills covering pipe materials, basic systems, and code principles. Students build and test simple plumbing assemblies in the lab.

  • Pipe materials: copper, PVC, CPVC, PEX, ABS
  • Cutting, joining, and fitting techniques for each material
  • Introduction to drain-waste-vent (DWV) systems
  • Water supply systems: hot and cold rough-in
  • Basic fixture installation: sink, toilet, shower rough-in
💧 Plumbing Track
HVAC I
HVAC track · Year 1
▾

Introduction to heating, cooling, and ventilation systems. Students learn refrigeration theory, system components, and basic airflow principles using training equipment in the lab.

  • The refrigeration cycle: compression, condensation, expansion, evaporation
  • Identifying system components: compressor, condenser, evaporator
  • Airflow fundamentals and duct types
  • Basic electrical controls: thermostats and contactors
  • Introduction to EPA 608 refrigerant regulations
🌀 HVAC Track
Carpentry I
Carpentry track · Year 1
▾

Core carpentry skills starting with lumber, layout, and structural framing. Students build wall sections, floor assemblies, and roof components in the lab.

  • Lumber grades, sizes, and species identification
  • Layout: snapping lines, reading plans, marking studs
  • Floor framing: joists, beams, and subfloor
  • Wall framing: plates, studs, headers, and corners
  • Roof framing basics and sheathing installation
🔨 Carpentry Track
Construction Business & Professionalism
Both semesters · All tracks
▾

The soft skills of the trades — employer expectations, workplace communication, and business fundamentals every tradesperson needs whether they work for someone else or themselves.

  • Professionalism: attendance, punctuality, and workplace conduct
  • Communicating with supervisors, coworkers, and customers
  • Understanding pay stubs, taxes, and benefits
  • Introduction to union vs. open-shop employment
  • Resume building and interview preparation
Core · All Tracks
Residential Construction Lab
Shared project-based lab
▾

A collaborative lab where students from all four tracks work together on a simulated residential construction project — mirroring the real-world experience of multiple trades on one job site.

  • Each trade completes their portion of a model residential structure
  • Students learn to coordinate with other trades' drawings
  • Introduction to construction sequencing and scheduling
  • Final walkthrough with instructor critique — simulates an inspection
Core · All Tracks
11th Grade
Intermediate Training + Workplace Experience
Deeper technical skills with job-site simulation. All students complete structured job-shadowing (minimum 60 hours) with a Cleveland-area partner. Multi-trade students add their second trade.
Electrical II
Electrical track · Year 2
▾

Builds directly on Electrical I with more complex wiring systems, conduit work, and panel installation. Students work from full residential plans and complete a wired model structure.

  • Conduit bending: offsets, saddles, and box offsets
  • EMT, rigid, and flexible conduit installation
  • Service entrance and panel installation
  • Branch circuit layout and load calculations
  • GFCI and AFCI requirements under NEC
  • Introduction to low-voltage: data and communication
⚡ Electrical Track
Plumbing II
Plumbing track · Year 2
▾

Advances into gas systems, water heating, and code-based system design. Students complete a full rough-in of a model bathroom and kitchen on a residential project.

  • Gas line materials, sizing, and pressure testing
  • Tank and tankless water heater installation
  • Venting systems: individual, common, and wet venting
  • Isometric drawing: reading and sketching plumbing diagrams
  • Code research: using the Ohio Plumbing Code to size systems
💧 Plumbing Track
HVAC II
HVAC track · Year 2
▾

Moves into refrigerant handling, residential load calculations, and troubleshooting. Students work with live training systems and begin preparing for the EPA 608 exam.

  • Refrigerant types, handling, and recovery procedures
  • Manifold gauge set use and system charging
  • Manual J residential load calculation basics
  • Troubleshooting using temperature-pressure relationships
  • Gas furnace operation, ignition systems, and heat exchangers
  • EPA 608 exam preparation (Type I, II, and Universal)
🌀 HVAC Track
Carpentry II
Carpentry track · Year 2
▾

Transitions from rough framing to interior finish work — the higher-skill, higher-wage side of carpentry. Students build stair stringers, install doors and windows, and complete interior trim packages.

  • Stair layout: rise, run, and stringer calculation
  • Door and window installation: plumb, level, and square
  • Interior trim: baseboard, casing, and crown molding
  • Cabinet installation: layout, shimming, and scribing
  • Introduction to hardwood flooring installation
🔨 Carpentry Track
Job-Shadow / Work-Based Learning
60 hrs minimum · All students
▾

Every 11th-grade student spends a minimum of 60 hours on a real job site with a Cleveland-area contractor or union partner — connecting classroom learning to professional reality.

  • Placed with a partner employer matched to their trade track
  • Supervised by a journeyman or foreman mentor
  • Students complete a structured observation journal
  • Midpoint and final evaluation by site supervisor
  • Top performers may receive early internship offers for 12th grade
Required · All Tracks
Estimating & Project Management
Semester 2 · All tracks
▾

Introduces the business side of construction — how jobs get priced, scheduled, and managed. Essential knowledge for anyone who wants to advance into a lead, foreman, or owner role.

  • Material takeoffs: counting and measuring from plans
  • Labor and material cost estimation
  • Bid documents: proposals, change orders, and invoices
  • Introduction to project scheduling: Gantt charts and sequencing
  • Using spreadsheet tools for job costing
Core · All Tracks
NCCER Level 1 Credential
End of 11th grade · Milestone
▾

The NCCER Level 1 credential is earned at the end of 11th grade, validating two years of trade-specific technical training.

  • Written knowledge assessment per trade
  • Hands-on performance evaluation by credentialed instructor
  • Portable credential recognized by contractors and unions nationwide
  • Counts toward apprenticeship application credit with IBEW, UA, and UBC
🏅 Credential Milestone
12th Grade
Advanced Skills + Apprenticeship Readiness
Advanced trade coursework, a 200-hour paid internship, capstone project, and a full credential stack at graduation. Every student walks the stage with a next step confirmed.
Electrical III (Advanced)
Electrical track · Year 3
▾

Advanced residential and commercial electrical systems. Students work from commercial blueprints and learn motor control basics — defining skills for a strong apprenticeship candidate.

  • Commercial wiring methods: wireways, cable trays, and MC cable
  • Three-phase power systems: theory and application
  • Motor controls: starters, contactors, and overloads
  • Transformer basics: step-up, step-down, and sizing
  • Fire alarm and emergency lighting systems introduction
⚡ Electrical Track
Plumbing III (Advanced)
Plumbing track · Year 3
▾

Commercial plumbing systems, larger-scale pipe sizing, and advanced code application. Students work from commercial drawings and practice skills required for apprenticeship entry.

  • Commercial DWV: larger pipe sizing and building drains
  • Storm drainage systems and roof drains
  • Water hammer, pressure reducing valves, and expansion tanks
  • Reading commercial plumbing plans and specifications
  • Permit application process and inspection simulation
💧 Plumbing Track
HVAC III (Advanced)
HVAC track · Year 3
▾

Commercial HVAC systems, advanced controls, and EPA 608 completion. Covers rooftop units, split systems, and the energy-efficiency technologies reshaping the trade.

  • Commercial rooftop units: installation and startup procedures
  • Variable air volume (VAV) systems introduction
  • Building automation and smart controls basics
  • Heat pump technology: air-source and geothermal overview
  • EPA 608 Universal exam — students sit and earn certification
🏅 Earns: EPA 608 Universal Certification
Carpentry III (Advanced)
Carpentry track · Year 3
▾

Commercial carpentry methods, advanced finish work, and project leadership skills. Students are expected to function at a near-apprentice level by year-end.

  • Metal stud framing for commercial interiors
  • Suspended ceiling systems: grid and tile installation
  • Advanced millwork: built-ins, wainscoting, coffered ceilings
  • Reading commercial architectural drawings
  • Lead carpenter role: planning, sequencing, directing helpers
🔨 Carpentry Track
Paid Internship / Pre-Apprenticeship
200 hrs · Employer partner
▾

A structured, paid placement with a Cleveland-area contractor, union training center, or facilities employer. Students work as paid helpers alongside journeymen for a minimum of 200 hours.

  • Matched to trade track and geographic preference
  • Paid at minimum wage or above — employer sets rate
  • Formal mentor assigned: journeyman or lead tradesperson
  • Hours count toward apprenticeship application credit with most unions
  • Top performers may receive full-time offers upon graduation
Required for Graduation · All Tracks
Capstone Project
Real build · Portfolio + presentation
▾

Each 12th-grade student completes a significant real-world build project demonstrating the full scope of their trade skills, presented to a panel of industry professionals.

  • Projects drawn from community-benefit builds (tiny home, facility upgrade)
  • Student produces written plan, material list, and budget before starting
  • Photo and video portfolio of the build process
  • Final presentation to union reps, contractors, and instructors
  • Portfolio becomes part of the student's job/apprenticeship application packet
Core · All Tracks
NCCER Level 2 + Trade Certification
OSHA-30 · EPA 608 (HVAC)
▾

The graduation-year credential stack. Students complete NCCER Level 2, earn OSHA-30, and (for HVAC) sit for EPA 608 Universal — one of the strongest entry-level packages in Northeast Ohio.

  • NCCER Level 2: advanced knowledge and performance assessment
  • OSHA-30: 30-hour construction safety certification
  • EPA 608 Universal (HVAC track only)
  • All credentials are portable, nationally recognized, and lifelong
🏅 Graduation Credential Stack
Apprenticeship / Job-Ready Placement
Graduation milestone
▾

Every graduate exits with an active placement — a union apprenticeship application submitted, a job offer in hand, or a community college dual-enrollment plan in place. No student walks the stage without a next step confirmed.

  • CMSD career coordinator works with each student by February of senior year
  • Union hall introductions facilitated through partner MOUs
  • Employer job fair held each spring for graduating seniors
  • 90-day and 1-year alumni check-ins to track outcomes
