The Challenge

Why CPP Must Act Now

Cleveland Public Power's infrastructure, much of it over a century old, is under severe strain. A 2024 infrastructure assessment and recent substation failures have exposed systemic vulnerabilities — creating urgency for a bold, technology-first overhaul.

⚡

Aging Infrastructure

CPP's 36 substations and distribution network — some components dating back to the 1900s — face mounting failures. A 2024 assessment pegged repair needs at $70–100 million, and the June 2025 Lake Road substation battery fire underscored how fragile the system has become.

📉

Grid Dependency

CPP obtains the majority of its power through short- and long-term contracts with regional suppliers via three 138 kV interconnections. This dependency exposes Cleveland ratepayers to volatile market pricing and single-point failure risk — both correctable with distributed generation.

🏚️

Reliability Crisis

A leaked 2020 consultant's report characterized CPP as an outlier among peer utilities, citing challenges in rates, reliability, and infrastructure competitiveness. Residents and businesses have voiced reliability concerns for years, threatening the utility's customer base.

🌿

Emissions & Sustainability

Cleveland's "green city on a blue lake" commitment requires the city's own utility to lead on clean energy. Conventional grid-tied power procurement limits CPP's ability to control its emissions profile or transition toward hydrogen and renewable fuels at scale.

💸

Rising Demand

Nationwide electricity demand is surging — driven by data centers, EV adoption, and industrial electrification — outpacing conventional grid capacity. CPP must plan not just for today's 74,000 customers, but for the Cleveland of 2035 and beyond.

🏛️

Political Momentum

Mayor Bibb's administration is preparing a transformation plan for CPP. City Council's Utilities Committee chair has publicly backed a comprehensive infrastructure overhaul. The political window is open — and the Hyliion KARNO solution fits precisely within it.

The Solution

Hyliion KARNO Technology

The KARNO generator is a revolutionary linear electric generator that converts heat into electricity through a flameless, low-temperature oxidation process — building on 200-year-old Stirling thermodynamic principles with cutting-edge aerospace engineering and additive manufacturing.

How KARNO Works

  • Uses heat to expand working fluid within a hermetically sealed cavity, pushing a piston assembly through a magnetic field to generate electric current — similar to a linear free-piston Stirling cycle.
  • Four synchronized shafts drive the thermodynamic cycle. With only 4 moving parts, KARNO eliminates the complexity of conventional combustion engines.
  • An external thermal reactor — entirely separate from the working fluid — enables flameless, low-temperature oxidation, producing ultra-low NOx and CO with zero carbon emissions on select fuels.
  • Fully enclosed and hermetically sealed: no emissions escape during normal operation. No additional treatment systems needed.
  • Successfully demonstrated grid-tied power delivery in Ohio — validating its viability for exactly this type of distributed municipal application.

Key Advantages for CPP

  • Scalable: KARNO is engineered to scale from 200 kW to multiple megawatts — right-sized for individual CPP substations.
  • Fuel-agnostic: Operates on 20+ fuels. CPP can source the cheapest or cleanest fuel available at any time, without shutdown or recalibration.
  • Load-following: Provides dispatchable power on demand with exceptional load-following capability — critical for peak shaving at substations.
  • Low maintenance: With only 4 moving parts, KARNO significantly reduces downtime and maintenance costs versus gas turbines or ICE generators.
  • Quiet & urban-ready: Quiet operation and compact design make KARNO ideal for urban substation environments throughout Cleveland's neighborhoods.

Upgrade Scope

All 36 CPP Substations

CPP's 36 substations receive 138 kV power and step it down to distribution voltages of 69 kV, 13.8 kV, 11.5 kV, and 2,300 V. Each will be upgraded with Hyliion KARNO Power Modules sized to meet local demand — enabling distributed generation, peak shaving, and grid-independent fallback capability.

Why This Works

Benefits for Cleveland

01

Grid Independence

On-site KARNO generation at each substation reduces CPP's dependence on three 138 kV interconnections, eliminating single-point failure risk and protecting ratepayers from regional grid pricing volatility.

02

Ultra-Low Emissions

KARNO's flameless oxidation process produces ultra-low NOx and CO without additional treatment systems. As Cleveland transitions to hydrogen fuel supply, zero-carbon operation becomes achievable across all 36 sites.

03

Drastic Maintenance Savings

With only four moving parts per generator, KARNO eliminates the complexity of combustion turbines and ICE systems. Reduced downtime and lower service costs free CPP resources for system expansion.

04

Peak Shaving & Net Metering

Distributed KARNO generation enables CPP to supply localized power during peak demand periods, reducing purchased power costs and enabling net metering opportunities that lower rates for Cleveland residents.

05

Future-Proof Fuel Flexibility

As Cleveland's gas infrastructure evolves — with hydrogen pipelines, biogas capture from NEORSD, and waste-gas recovery — KARNO modules can switch fuels without shutdown or recalibration, protecting the city's investment for decades.

06

Local Reliability Wins

Neighborhood-level generation means a failure at one substation doesn't cascade. The Lake Road battery fire of 2025 — which knocked out 3,000+ customers — would be mitigated by on-site KARNO backup capacity.

Implementation Roadmap

Phased Deployment

A four-phase rollout aligns with CPP's operational reality — keeping the grid stable while progressively decarbonizing and fortifying each substation across Cleveland's neighborhoods.

1
// Years 1–2

Assessment & Pilot

Commission a full engineering study of all 36 substations. Deploy pilot KARNO modules at 2–3 high-priority sites (including Lake Road). Establish baseline performance data, fuel supply contracts, and PUCO permitting framework.

2
// Years 2–4

Tier 1 Rollout

Install KARNO Power Modules at the 12 highest-load substations serving Cleveland's most critical residential and commercial corridors. Begin peak shaving operations. Integrate with CPP's existing SCADA system for unified fleet monitoring.

3
// Years 4–7

Full Fleet Deployment

Complete installation across all remaining 24 substations. Scale from natural gas operation toward hydrogen and biogas blends as Cleveland's alternative fuel infrastructure matures. Achieve net metering capabilities system-wide.

4
// Year 7+

Grid Independence

Operate the full 36-substation KARNO fleet as a distributed generation network capable of islanding critical city infrastructure. Transition high-confidence substations to hydrogen fuel, achieving zero-carbon milestones aligned with Cleveland's sustainability commitments.
