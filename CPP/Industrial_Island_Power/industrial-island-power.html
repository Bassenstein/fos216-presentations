CONCEPT OVERVIEW

Campus-islanded power at 800 VDC — eliminating conversion losses and creating an energy-sovereign data campus for Cleveland's Industrial Valley.

The Industrial Island Power concept replaces the conventional AC distribution model — with its cascading rectifier, UPS, and transformer losses — with a single 800 VDC backbone that connects every generation source directly to every load. The result is fewer conversion stages, lower wire losses, and a system that can operate fully islanded from the utility grid.

800 VDC is chosen deliberately: high enough to reduce conductor losses across long data hall runs on a 35-acre campus, yet below the 1,000 VDC threshold that triggers more stringent NEC code requirements and OSHA hazard tiers. At the rack level, modern Titanium-rated server PSUs accept 400 VDC directly — one DC-DC step from the 800 V bus at 97–98% efficiency.

The architecture is conceived in three integrated layers: the KARNO distributed generation array as the primary mid-layer generation source, BESS as instantaneous transient coverage, and the GE Vernova 7HA.01 gas turbine as bulk baseload — with a future pathway for SMR integration at the same AFE interface without bus redesign.

03

GENERATION STACK

Three generation layers create a resilient, self-healing power stack. Each layer serves a distinct temporal role — from millisecond transient coverage to 20-minute bulk ramp — with no single point of failure. The architecture is designed so that no rotating machine needs to respond to instantaneous load steps; the BESS absorbs all transients before any generator needs to react.
LAYER 01 · INSTANTANEOUS
BESS
DC-Coupled Battery Storage
~0 ms response
Always-on DC bus connection — no transfer switching, no rectifier/inverter stack between storage and load. Absorbs all instantaneous load steps and generator governor lag. Sized to cover the KARNO thermal ramp window from a running operating point. In a campus-islanded architecture, BESS is the voltage reference on the bus; all other sources follow it. Recharges from KARNO and turbine during normal operation.
LAYER 02 · MID-LAYER
KARNO
Hyliion KARNO 200 kW Arrays
4 min cold · hot-standby faster
Primary distributed generation layer. Arrays run continuously at 40–60% partial load as baseload distributed generation, enabling faster load-following response from a running thermal state than the published 4-minute cold-start figure. Each 200 kW unit operates as a fuel-agnostic DC source. 50-unit array yields 10 MW of distributed generation. 2 MW KARNO modules (anticipated 2026) will simplify array management. Fuel transitions on-the-fly — natural gas today, hydrogen blend as on-site electrolysis scales.
LAYER 03 · BASELOAD
7HA.01
GE Vernova Simple-Cycle Turbine
10–20 min governor ramp
Bulk baseload and campus-islanding anchor. Medium-voltage AC output steps down through transformer then through AFE rectifier onto the 800 VDC bus — same AFE interface that will accommodate a future SMR generator without bus redesign. Handles sustained high-load conditions that exceed KARNO array capacity. Governor response is slow but the BESS/KARNO combination covers the gap completely. Future fuel path: hydrogen co-fire as on-site H₂ production scales.

DC Output — Bus Integration Opportunity
The KARNO natively offers AC or DC output. For the 800 VDC bus, the preferred mode is DC-coupled via internal power electronics — bypassing the optional grid-tie inverter entirely and injecting DC directly. This eliminates one full conversion stage. The grid-tie inverter is only required for AC-bus or utility interconnect scenarios. Integration with the campus 800 VDC bus requires confirmation of the internal DC link voltage from Hyliion's applications team.
Flameless Oxidation — OEPA Permit Advantage
The KARNO uses continuous low-temperature flameless oxidation rather than intermittent high-pressure combustion. This produces inherently low NOx without catalytic aftertreatment. For a Cuyahoga County campus, this significantly simplifies the Title V air permit pathway and may allow the array to remain below Best Available Control Technology review thresholds — a meaningful timeline and cost advantage over diesel or conventional gas generator alternatives.
Ramp Rate Context — Cold vs. Running
The 4-minute cold-start figure applies to a unit starting from ambient. In a prime-power campus architecture, KARNO arrays run continuously at partial load — load-following from a running thermal state is substantially faster. The January 2026 Navy testing validated rapid load-change capability under mission-representative conditions, though specific hot-state ramp rates haven't been published. BESS is sized to cover the gap regardless. Hot standby mode further reduces reactivation time.
Engineering Questions for Hyliion
1. What is the internal DC link voltage — can it interface directly to an 800 VDC bus?

2. What is the quantified hot-standby ramp rate from a running partial-load condition?

3. Minimum array size for stable islanded DC bus voltage participation — N=1 or N≥3?

4. Does control system export a grid-forming or grid-following signal for DC bus voltage regulation?

HYDROGEN PATHWAY

The KARNO's fuel-agnostic architecture enables a phased transition from natural gas to hydrogen without hardware changes. As on-site electrolysis capacity grows — powered by campus renewable or SMR generation — the KARNO fuel mix shifts progressively. The Industrial Island Power campus can host an internal hydrogen economy: electrolysis produces H₂, KARNO arrays consume it, the 800 VDC bus delivers the electrons.
PHASE 01 · NOW
Natural Gas Prime Power
100% NG
KARNO arrays commission on pipeline natural gas. Campus achieves grid independence and 800 VDC islanding. OEPA air permits established under low-NOx flameless oxidation profile. Electrolyzer infrastructure permitted and partially installed.
PHASE 02 · NEAR-TERM
Hydrogen Blend Introduction
NG + H₂ BLEND
On-site electrolyzer comes online powered by excess campus generation. KARNO software automatically detects fuel blend and adjusts flow rates — no hardware modification. Blend ratio increases as electrolyzer capacity scales. Same KARNO units, same DC interface, cleaner output.
PHASE 03 · MID-TERM
Majority Hydrogen Operation
H₂ DOMINANT
Electrolyzer produces the majority of KARNO fuel demand on-site. Excess H₂ stored or sold. SMR addition (if approved) provides large-scale zero-carbon electricity for deeper electrolysis. 7HA.01 transitions to H₂ co-fire. Campus carbon intensity drops sharply.
PHASE 04 · LONG-TERM
Zero-Carbon Campus
100% H₂ / ZERO-C
Full hydrogen-fueled KARNO operation. SMR provides baseload zero-carbon generation. 7HA.01 retains on standby for grid services and emergency. Campus qualifies for zero-carbon compute power premium pricing. Industrial Valley becomes a hydrogen energy hub for Northeast Ohio.
06

SMR FUTURE PATH

SMR
READY
Nuclear · Zero Carbon · Baseload

The 800 VDC bus architecture is designed from the outset to accept a Small Modular Reactor as a future generation source with no redesign of the campus bus infrastructure. An SMR — such as NuScale's VOYGR (~77 MWe per module at ~13.8 kV) — produces medium-voltage 3-phase AC from a steam turbine-generator. That output routes through a step-down transformer and AFE rectifier onto the 800 VDC bus, identical to the 7HA.01 interface already in the design.

The SMR's operating profile is particularly well-suited to this architecture: reactors prefer near-constant output (baseload), and the BESS/KARNO combination handles variable compute load gracefully, absorbing load steps that the reactor's turbine governor hasn't yet responded to. No diesel backup is required — the reactor is the baseload island, and BESS handles transients.

An additional integration opportunity exists: SMR waste heat can drive on-site hydrogen electrolysis (high-temperature electrolysis is significantly more efficient than ambient-temperature PEM) or provide process heat for facility systems — extending the campus energy efficiency beyond the electrical conversion chain alone.

The SMR pathway is not a redesign. It is an addition — same AFE, same bus, same KARNO arrays, same BESS. The campus grows its zero-carbon baseload capacity while the distributed generation layer and battery layer remain unchanged.
