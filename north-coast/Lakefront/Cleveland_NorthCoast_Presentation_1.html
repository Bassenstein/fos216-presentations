The moment

Cleveland's lakefront
has waited long enough.

The Huntington Bank Stadium will be removed by 2030, freeing the most significant undeveloped urban lakefront parcel in the Midwest. This is not just a real estate opportunity. It is Cleveland's chance to build a destination that reflects who we truly are — a city with a soundtrack, a scientific curiosity, and a lake that belongs to everyone.

01
The right site

Steps from the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, adjacent to the Great Lakes Science Center, and directly connected to downtown via the Mall C corridor. No other city has a lakefront parcel with these neighbors, this visibility, and this civic significance.

02
The right moment

Stadium removal, major federal infrastructure funding availability, and a city-county alignment around lakefront development create a window that may not open again for a generation. The time to act is now — with a bold, unified vision.

03
The right idea

A world-first Science of Bass Museum. The permanent home of the Rock Hall Induction Ceremony. A land bridge that finally connects downtown to the water. No other American city can build exactly this — because no other city has Cleveland's story to tell.

04
Year-round economic engine

Not a seasonal attraction. Not a stadium that sits dark 300 days a year. A campus designed for daily activation — museum visitors, recreation, food, outdoor events, concerts, school groups, tourists, and Clevelanders who finally have a reason to walk to their own lakefront.

Anchor destination

Rock Hall Performance Center

Cleveland will never again send the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame Induction Ceremony to New York. The Rock Hall Performance Center becomes the permanent, iconic home of the most celebrated night in music — and one of the most versatile event venues in the Midwest.

Facing Lake Erie to the north with a full-height glass wall, the building is a landmark visible from the water and from the highway — a civic gesture that says Cleveland takes its cultural identity seriously.

Why Cleveland owns this

The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame has held induction ceremonies in New York and Los Angeles since 1986. The museum has been in Cleveland since 1995. It is long past time for the ceremony to come home permanently.

A purpose-built venue designed around the ceremony — with broadcast infrastructure, artist facilities, and the full production capability of a world-class performance space — makes Cleveland the undisputed capital of rock and roll, not just its museum city.

  • —Six artist dressing suites and full green room
  • —Rock Hall exhibit concourse throughout
  • —Retractable floor seating for full versatility
Signature programming

Two world-class events.
One unforgettable campus.

The North Coast campus isn't just a collection of buildings. It is a platform for the events that define Cleveland as the music capital of the Midwest. Two anchor conferences — the Midwest Music Conference and the Great Lakes Music Gathering — bring thousands of artists, industry professionals, and music fans to the campus every year, filling every venue, every hotel room, and every restaurant in the city.

Annual conference
Midwest Music Conference
The premier industry gathering for music professionals across the Midwest — labels, managers, booking agents, publishers, producers, and emerging artists who want to be discovered. Modeled on Austin's SXSW and Nashville's Americana Fest, the MMC uses the entire North Coast campus as its conference center, with the Performance Center as its anchor venue and the Science of Bass Museum as its unmistakable identity.

Annual festival
Great Lakes Music Gathering
A public-facing multi-day music festival celebrating the full musical spectrum of the Great Lakes region — from Cleveland's deep soul and gospel roots to Detroit's techno legacy, Chicago blues, Cincinnati funk, and the emerging scenes from across Ohio, Michigan, and Indiana. The Gathering is the community counterpart to the MMC — free and low-cost programming that makes the North Coast a destination for every Clevelander, not just the music industry.

World's first

The Science of
Bass Museum

No museum anywhere in the world has claimed bass as its subject. The science of low-frequency sound touches everything — nature, the human body, music history, engineering, and culture. Cleveland is the only city that can tell this story with complete authority. And we will.

3rd
Make it
The Recording Lab
A fully working recording studio open to students, community members, and school groups. Real equipment, real credentials, real careers launched.
Bass Engineering Lab
Hands-on exploration of how speakers, amplifiers, and sound systems actually work — from circuit boards to concert-scale systems.
Audio Partner Innovation Suite
A permanent 8,000 sq ft gallery co-designed with our world-class audio equipment partner — showing cutting-edge sound technology in action.
2nd
Hear it
Bass in Nature
Whale song, earthquake rumble, Lake Erie acoustics, thunder. The natural world is full of bass — visitors listen through bone-conduction headphones to feel what they're hearing.
Bass in Culture
From African talking drums to New Orleans tuba to hip-hop's 808 drum machine. A journey through humanity's deepest relationship with low frequency sound.
Cleveland Artists Wing
Rotating gallery celebrating Northeast Ohio's extraordinary musical legacy — from Motown soul to funk, gospel, metal, and beyond.
1st
Feel it
The Haptic Chamber
You feel bass before you understand it. A sealed room where subsonic frequencies travel through the floor, walls, and air. Whale calls. Thunderstorms. The opening notes of a symphony. Unforgettable.
The Waveform Gallery
Sound waves made visible. Giant interactive installations reveal how frequency, amplitude, and resonance work — science you can see and touch.
Body and Bass
Why does bass feel different in your chest than treble does in your ears? The biology of low-frequency perception — from inner ear anatomy to why certain sounds make you want to move.
Audio Equipment Partner — Founding Partnership

The Science of Bass Museum is anchored by a world-class professional audio equipment company as a Founding Partner. Their technology powers every listening experience in the building — from the Haptic Chamber to the Recording Lab — and their Innovation Suite on the third floor is the most technically sophisticated exhibit space in the museum. This is a partnership built on shared passion for sound. Their equipment doesn't just play music in the building. It is the subject of the building. That alignment creates something no amount of sponsorship money alone can buy: authenticity.

Connecting the city

The Land Bridge.
Downtown meets the lake.

For decades, Cleveland's lakefront has been separated from its downtown by a rail corridor that felt insurmountable. The Land Bridge ends that separation permanently — a landscaped, pedestrian and cycling crossing that makes the North Coast campus a genuine extension of downtown, not an island you drive to.

The path forward

From vision to
opening night.

Phase 1 · 2026 Q2–Q3
Alignment and commitment
All three Founding Partners execute a Letter of Intent. A joint Steering Committee is formed. The City of Cleveland and Cuyahoga County are formally engaged. A master planning architect is retained. Federal and state pre-development funding applications are filed.
Phase 2 · 2026 Q4–2027 Q2
Planning and capital campaign launch
Financial pro forma completed. Partnership agreement executed. Exhibit design firm selected for the Science of Bass Museum. Capital campaign publicly launched. Ohio Cultural Facilities Commission application submitted. Community engagement begins.
Phase 3 · 2027 Q3–2028 Q4
Design and documentation
Full architectural design development for all buildings and site. Audio equipment partner finalizes all system specifications. Exhibit fabrication contracts awarded. Construction documents completed. Permits filed. Pre-construction begins.
Phase 4 · 2029–2030
Construction
Site work begins immediately following stadium removal. Building construction proceeds in parallel with exhibit fabrication and installation. Land Bridge construction coordinated with City infrastructure schedule. BASSBOSS systems installed and commissioned.
Phase 5 · 2030 Q4
Opening
Soft opening for Founding Partners, members, and community preview. Public grand opening of the Science of Bass Museum and North Coast Campus. First Rock Hall Induction Ceremony in the new Performance Center. Cleveland reclaims its lakefront — permanently.
