The Program
Three Pillars
Every BASS Museum event is built on three interlocking pillars that together make it unlike anything else on the civic calendar.
01
Live Sound
World-class bass music artists. Sub-bass rigs that redefine what a Cleveland festival sounds like. Curated from underground to headliner — every event a different lineup, one consistent standard of sonic excellence. Cardioid-array sub systems engineered for outdoor civic space. The kind of sound most cities only hear in arenas.
02
Science of Bass
Interactive exhibits on acoustics, psychoacoustics, and the physics of low-frequency sound — education embedded in the live experience. The Science of Bass Museum lives inside every event: portable versions of the haptic chamber, waveform gallery, and bass-in-nature installations activate the footprint between sets. The museum is the event. The event is the museum.
03
Cultural Roots
Cleveland's contribution to bass-driven music — from blues and gospel to funk, hip-hop, and electronic — honored, documented, and celebrated live. Rotating curatorial focus spotlights a different chapter of Cleveland's sonic history at each event. This is not just a concert. It is a cultural institution with an editorial point of view.
The Site
Mall C —
Cleveland's
Civic Stage
Mall C (Strawbridge Plaza) is one of Cleveland's most underutilized civic assets. Designed for gathering. Built for scale. Located at the heart of downtown, surrounded by the infrastructure of a major American city — transit, hotels, restaurants, parking, and institutions.

BASS Museum activates this space across five defined zones: a main stage at the heart anchored by a world-class sub-bass rig, the Bass Museum Gallery, a vendor village of 40+ local businesses, a community stage for emerging artists, and sponsor activation areas spanning the full footprint.

Five thousand people. In the civic center. Celebrating Cleveland.

The World's First
Science of
Bass Museum
No museum anywhere in the world has claimed bass as its subject. The science of low-frequency sound touches everything — nature, the human body, music history, engineering, and culture. Cleveland is the only city that can tell this story with complete authority.

A joint venture between the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame, the Great Lakes Science Center, and a world-class audio equipment partner. Three floors. Three experiences.
3
MAKE IT
The Recording Lab
A fully working recording studio open to students, community members, and school groups. Real equipment, real credentials, real careers launched. Tied to CMSD and Tri-C workforce pipelines.
Bass Engineering Lab
Hands-on exploration of how speakers, amplifiers, and sound systems actually work — from circuit boards to concert-scale systems. Build your own speaker. Tune your own subwoofer.
Audio Partner Innovation Suite
A permanent 8,000 sq ft gallery co-designed with our world-class audio equipment partner — showing cutting-edge sound technology in action. Industry demos, product showcases, immersive listening rooms.
2
HEAR IT
Bass in Nature
Whale song, earthquake rumble, Lake Erie acoustics, thunder. The natural world is full of bass. Visitors listen through bone-conduction headphones to feel what they're hearing — and learn why low frequencies travel farther and carry more energy.
Bass in Culture
From African talking drums to New Orleans tuba to hip-hop's 808 drum machine. A musicological journey across centuries and continents, with listening stations, rare recordings, and the story of how bass became the backbone of modern music.
Cleveland Artists Wing
Rotating exhibits celebrating Northeast Ohio's contributions to bass-heavy music — from funk, gospel, and soul to industrial, electronic, and hip-hop. The city's own voice, amplified.
1
FEEL IT
The Haptic Chamber
A sealed room where visitors feel subsonic frequencies (20–80 Hz) through the floor, walls, and air — experiencing bass before they hear it. The signature exhibit. Nothing else like it in any museum in the world.
Waveform Gallery
Walk-through visualizations of sound waves, frequency, and resonance. See how bass behaves differently from treble in the human body and built environment. Real-time wave physics rendered at human scale.
Bass in the Body
The science of how low-frequency sound interacts with human physiology — heart rate, bone conduction, the vestibular system, crowd psychology, and why bass feels the way it feels at a live show.
City Impact
The Numbers
Are Clear
For the City of Cleveland and its sponsors, BASS Museum delivers measurable returns — modeled on comparable bass and electronic music festivals in peer cities, applied to Cleveland's existing infrastructure and visitor base.

The investment case is clear. The civic case is compelling. The cultural moment is now.
1,200 Hotel Nights Per Event ▼
Based on a 30% out-of-town attendee rate at 5,000 capacity — conservative modeling against comparables like Day Zero Detroit and Movement Electronic Music Festival. Nights filled at downtown Cleveland hotels within a 1-mile radius of Mall C.
40+ Local Vendors On-Site ▼
The vendor village is curated — no national chains, no franchises. All 40+ vendor slots reserved for Cleveland-based small businesses: food, drink, merchandise, art, and craft. Priority given to businesses from underserved neighborhoods.
200 Jobs Created ▼
Across production, hospitality, security, logistics, vendor staffing, and event operations — per-event employment equivalent to a mid-sized concert with the add-on of museum and vendor staffing layers unique to BASS Museum's format.
5M Annual Media Impressions ▼
Organic social reach + press coverage + sponsor amplification + artist promo across 12 events. Bass music has one of the most engaged online fan bases in live music — cross-platform reach compounds significantly with a recurring-event model.
$2M+ Annual Direct Local Spending ▼
Ticket revenue retained locally, vendor sales, hotel spend, restaurant and bar spend within the event radius. Does not include secondary economic activity or multiplier effects from repeat visitation and tourism growth year-over-year.

Signature Programming
Annual Events
Beyond the monthly BASS Museum events, two signature annual gatherings elevate the program to national and industry significance — cementing Cleveland's position as the music capital of the Midwest.
March · Annual
Midwest Music Conference
A credentialed industry conference held at the Huntington Convention Center — two days of programming across six tracks: Business, Technology, Culture, Education, Policy, and Innovation. Modeled on Miami's Winter Music Conference.

Brings together artists, engineers, promoters, label executives, educators, and civic leaders from across the region and beyond. Press-credentialed. Speaker submissions open six months out.

August · Annual
Great Lakes Music Gathering
A public-facing two-day lakefront event anchored at the North Coast Village — science presentations and keynotes on Friday at the Great Lakes Science Center, followed by a full multi-stage music festival on Saturday, closing with fireworks over Lake Erie.

The civic face of the entire BASS Museum enterprise — open, free or low-cost, family-oriented by day and full-spectrum by night. Peak single-day attendance projected at 15,000–20,000.

Founding Partnership
Built Together
The Science of Bass Museum is structured as a joint venture between three founding institutions — each bringing irreplaceable credibility, infrastructure, and audience to the table. No single institution could tell this story alone.
Rock & Roll Hall of Fame
Cultural Authority & Programming
The world's preeminent institution for the preservation and celebration of rock history. Brings curatorial expertise, national audience, existing education programs, and the annual Induction Ceremony — which BASS Museum positions to return permanently to Cleveland.
Great Lakes Science Center
Scientific Credibility & STEM Pipeline
Cleveland's premier science institution and NASA Glenn regional visitor center. Brings exhibition design expertise, STEM education infrastructure, school district relationships, and the scientific framework that makes the Bass Museum a legitimate science destination — not just a music venue.
Audio Equipment Partner
Technology & Innovation Suite
A world-class professional audio manufacturer whose systems set the global standard for sub-bass reproduction. Brings cutting-edge technology, the 8,000 sq ft Innovation Suite, live system design expertise, and the industry credibility that makes the Bass Engineering Lab a real training ground — not a display case.
